“What excites me about design?”

My passion for solving problems was apparent since my high school times, even though they were just the academic ones then. After completing my undergrad in Computer Engineering from Mumbai (India) I started to work as a Software Developer at Infosys. At work, I was always complimented for being able to look at a bigger picture, a skill that I guess I picked up in my very first project. Later I joined JP Morgan, where while working in a fast-paced environment I found it exciting to analyze the problems, get to the root cause and provide solutions. I always ensured I understood the problem right and found better ways to solve them. Additionally, I learned to collaborate with multiple people - product managers, testers, salespeople and other developers.

Often at work, I was appreciated having strong attention to detail but it was only last year while helping a friend on his start-up, I discovered my interest in creating engaging, delightful and seamless experiences for the people to reach their goals. It was the first time I came across the term - User Experience (UX) and I started to read more about it and took a few online courses. Thereafter I decided to go to school and dive deeper into the field of Human-Computer Interaction.

Through all this, I’ve realized that my passion for design stems from a desire to make a lasting and profound positive impact. As a student at the University of Illinois Urbana Champaign, I am taught not only how to build a product for the user, but also how to analyze its intersections with society and the environment. It's a complicated world out there, which I would love to simplify.
